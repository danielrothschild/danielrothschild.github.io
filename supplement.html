A Puzzle about Knowing Conditionals

Daniel Rothschild and Levi Spectre

This is a short explicit version of the argument in “A Puzzle about Knowing Odds” in Noûs. This is intended as a supplement to the paper, not a self-standing note.

Notation:

Hn/Tn = nth coin land heads/tails.

H1…n = the coins from 1 to n all land heads.

Starting assumptions: (where n is used these are schematic for all n between 1 (or 2) and 1000)

  1. K(¬H1…1000) (Anti-skepticism)
  2. the probability of Hn = .5 (setup)
  3. the probability of H1…n − 1 → Tn = .5 (from Independence and Adam’s thesis and (2), this is only schematic for n > 1 for obvious reasons)
  4. [K(ϕ ∨ θ)∧¬Kϕ ∧ ¬Kθ]→K(¬ϕ → θ) (Restricted or-to-if)
  5. Kϕ→ the probability of ϕ is greater than .5 (Knowledge and probability)

Argument: (where n is used these are schematic for all n between 1 (or 2) and 1000)

  1. ¬KHn (from (2) and (5) by modus tollens)
  2. [K(¬H1…n − 1 ∨ Tn)∧¬KH1…n − 1 ∧ ¬KTn]→K(H1…n − 1 → Tn) (from (4) with double negation elimination)
  3. ¬K(¬H1…n − 1 → Tn) (from (3) and (5) by modus tollens)
  4. ¬[K(¬H1…n − 1 ∨ Tn)∧¬KH1…n − 1 ∧ ¬KTn] (from (7) and (8) by modus tollens)
  5. ¬[K(¬H1…999 ∨ Tn)∧¬KH1…999 ∧ ¬KT1000] (instance of (9))
  6. K(¬H1…999 ∨ Tn) (by (1) and closure of knowledge under logical equivalence)
  7. ¬(¬KH1…999 ∧ ¬KT1000) (from (10) by inference rule ¬(ϕ ∧ ψ),ϕ ⊨ ¬ψ)
  8. KH1…999 (by (12) and (6) using ¬(ϕ ∧ ψ),ϕ ⊨ ¬ψ and double negation elimination)
  9. KH1 (by repeating above reasoning 998 times)

The only inference rules we used here are double negation elimination, modus tollens, ¬(ϕ ∧ ψ),ϕ ⊨ ¬ψ. These are all valid in many non-classical logics where the LEM fails such as Strong Kleene and Łukasiewicz Logics.
